DIY Tax Guide for Tradies
File your own return · ATO myTax
1
Log in to myGov → ATO
Link your ATO account at my.gov.au. Pre-filled data auto-populates most fields.
2
Declare All Income
Include all invoices, cash jobs, and any other payments. Don't miss foreign income.
3
Claim All Deductions
Tools, vehicle, phone, home office, equipment, work clothing, insurance, training.
4
Lodge by 31 October
DIY deadline is 31 Oct. If you use a tax agent, you may get until May the following year.
⚠️ Note: Tax rules differ by visa type. Working Holiday Makers (417/462) are taxed at 15% from $1 — no tax-free threshold applies.

📖 What to Know Before You Start
🏛️ ASIC Regulation
All investment products in Australia must be regulated by ASIC. Only use products with an AFS Licence. Verify at moneysmart.gov.au before investing.
💸 Tax on Investments
Capital gains are taxed at your marginal rate. Assets held 12+ months get a 50% CGT discount. Dividends may include franking credits that reduce your tax.
💡 Emergency Fund First
Before investing, ensure you have 3–6 months of living expenses in a high-interest savings account. As a sole trader, income can be irregular.
🔄 Dollar-Cost Averaging
Investing the same amount regularly (weekly/monthly) reduces the impact of market volatility — you buy more when prices are low, less when high.
✈️ Your Immigration Status
Different visas have different tax rules in Australia
🇦🇺 Australian Citizen or Permanent Resident
Full tax-free threshold ($18,200). Standard resident tax rates apply.
💼 Skilled Work Visa (482, 457, 186)
Resident for tax purposes if staying 183+ days. Standard rates + Medicare levy.
🌿 Working Holiday Maker (417 / 462)
Taxed at 15% on first $45,000. No tax-free threshold. No Medicare levy.
🎓 Student Visa (500)
Max 48 hrs/fortnight work. Tax-free threshold applies if main income. GST/BAS rules same.
🏢 Business / Investor Visa (188, 888)
Resident for tax. Foreign income may be taxable. CGT and super obligations differ.
🌐 Other / Bridging / Spouse Visa
Tax residency depends on stay duration. Consult a registered tax agent for your situation.
